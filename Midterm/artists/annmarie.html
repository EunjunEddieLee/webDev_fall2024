Anne-Marie

Who is Anne-Marie

Anne-Marie is a British singer-songwriter. She started her music career by participating in the British band Rudimental's "Rumour Mill" and released her debut EP "Karate" in June 2015. The following year, she released her single "Do It Right" and reached No. 9 on the UK chart to make her name known, followed by "Clear Bandit, Sean Paul and Rockabye" to maintain her No. 1 spot on the UK chart for nine consecutive weeks. In 2018, she released her debut studio album "Speak Your Mind" and was nominated for four "2019 Brit Awards" along with the No. 3 spot on the UK album chart. In particular, her song "2002" gained explosive popularity in Korea, making her dominate various music charts and produce many domestic fans. In 2021, she released her second studio album " Therapy" in three years, and she delivered comforting music during the pandemic, including 12 songs about "Healing" as the title track "Beautiful" that Ed Sheeran and Max Martin collaborated on.

  • Therapy

    Therapy is the second studio album by English singer-songwriter Anne-Marie. It was released on 23 July 2021, through Major Tom's and Asylum Records. It features guest appearances by KSI, Digital Farm Animals, Little Mix, Niall Horan, Nathan Dawe, MoStack, and Rudimental. The album was supported by four singles: "Don't Play", "Way Too Long", "Our Song", and "Kiss My (Uh-Oh)".
