Thornapple

Who is Thornapple

Thornapple (Korean: 쏜애플) is a South Korean indie rock band. The band currently consists of Yoon Seonghyeon, Shim Jaehyeon, Hong Dongkyoon and Bang Joseph.[1] Since their formation in 2009, the band has released three studio albums: I Often Stammer and Forget the Way to Sleep (난 자꾸 말을 더듬고 잠드는 법도 잊었네) (2010), Strange Weather (이상기후) (2014) and Enlightenment (계몽) (2019).[2]

  • I Keep Stuttering And Have Forgotten How To Sleep

    Yoon Sung-hyun, the band's leader, called Thornapple's music "the song of adolescence, which does not end even after the age of 20." As he said, Thornapple's debut album [I keep stuttering and forgetting how to fall asleep] contains intimate insights into "I," "You," and "Communication," which are like teen fever that everyone experiences. In "Time of the Orange," it reveals the obsession within a human being and the desire for self-destruction that only humans can have, and in "Red Peter," a title reminiscent of Franz Kafka, it reveals the clash between the Lacan self and the corgito that can only be completed by a batter.

  • Strange Weather

    It is difficult to capture Thorne Apple's music, which is filled with explosive power, thirst, and aspiration that burns everything at once, on one album. Producer Seo Sang-eun's meeting was refined to express Thorne Apple's music to the maximum within the medium of the album. Survival is the keyword penetrating Thorne Apple's second album, "Strange Climate." All the songs come out in a polygonal form under one word. "Strange Climate" is a kind of concept album. It is a collection of short stories written on one theme, and a big "story" in which each chapter is organically intertwined and woven. There are many albums where the hooks of the songs are great or the musical structural similarities are unified. Such albums are also great. However, I can say that no band in the current band scene sings a single theme with 100% energy so acutely that it is "unfamiliar."
